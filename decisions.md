# Globetrotter — Decisions Log

## Milestone 0: Setup and Planning
- Destination chosen: Chengdu, China 
- Primary audience: first time chengdu traveler and panda Lovers 
- One design decision that reflects the destination: A place I personally always wanted to visit since pandas are my favorite animal
- Wireframe format used (hand-drawn / Figma / other): Figma 

## Milestone 1: HTML Structure
One HTML structure choice you made and why: Used `figure` + `figcaption` for gallery captions.
One thing Claude generated that you changed, and why: Changed the Chengdu logo from link to plain text to match wireframe.
One place where your wireframe guided a specific decision about structure: Kept one shared header/nav on all pages.

## Milestone 2: CSS Styling
One color or font choice you made, and why it serves your destination: Black/white base + bamboo green accents for panda theme.
One Claude suggestion you rejected, and why: Rejected replacing my image links; kept my original links.
One style that didn't look right at first, and what you changed: Cards looked too small, so I increased size and spacing.

## Milestone 3: Flexbox Layout
One Flexbox property choice you made deliberately, and why: Used `flex-wrap` to keep grid behavior responsive.
One place where Claude generated a layout that didn't match your plan, and what you changed: Attractions collapsed too soon, so I adjusted widths/breakpoints.
One layout challenge that required adjusting your HTML structure, and why: Kept gallery items in `figure` blocks so image + caption stay together.

## Milestone 4: Responsive Design
What breakpoints did you end up using, and why those values? Around 980px, 760px, 700px, and 680px, based on where layout got crowded.
One section where the mobile layout needed to feel genuinely different, and what you did: Gallery changed from 3/2 layout to one-column scroll on phone.
One Claude suggestion about breakpoints you accepted or rejected, and why: Accepted staged breakpoints, but edited values to better match my Figma.
